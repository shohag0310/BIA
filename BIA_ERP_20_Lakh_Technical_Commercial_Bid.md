# 1. Executive Proposal

This proposal offers a basic integrated ERP for BIA within a fixed implementation budget of BDT 20,00,000. The solution will establish one shared platform for employee administration, leave, attendance, payroll, basic accounts, inventory, membership, file tracking, notifications and essential reporting.

The proposed price is based on standardized workflows, controlled customization and strict limits on integrations, migration and reports. Advanced enterprise capabilities may be added later through separately approved upgrades without replacing the Phase 1 foundation.

# 2. Project Objectives

- Establish one centralized application for BIA's principal administrative functions.
- Digitize essential employee, leave, attendance and payroll processes.
- Record basic financial receipts, payments, vouchers and ledgers.
- Track inventory requisition, receipt, issue and assignment.
- Maintain member information, billing, payment and basic portal access.
- Register and track BIA files and supporting documents.
- Provide essential dashboards, reports, user permissions and activity history.
- Create an extensible technical foundation for later enterprise enhancements.

# 4. Basic Functional Scope

## 4.1 Platform and Administration

- One BIA organization and one operational policy set.
- Department, designation, employee category, holiday and general settings.
- Secure login and password management.
- Predefined Administrator, HR, Approver, Payroll, Accounts, Store, Membership, File Officer and Employee roles.
- Basic module/action permissions.
- Important user and transaction activity history.
- Responsive browser-based user interface.

## 4.2 HRM and Employee Profile

- Employee personal, contact and employment information.
- Present/permanent address, marital status, children/dependants, nominee and emergency contact.
- Department, designation, joining date, employee status and bank information.
- Photograph and selected employee-document upload.
- Employee list, search, profile view and Excel import/export.
- Basic joining, transfer, increment and separation history.

## 4.3 Leave Management

- Leave types and opening quotas.
- Online leave application.
- One-step approve/reject process.
- Leave balance and transaction history.
- Half-day leave.
- Basic email notification.
- Leave register and balance reports.

## 4.4 Attendance Management

- Integration with one documented attendance-device model at one site, subject to technical feasibility.
- Excel/CSV attendance import as an operational fallback.
- Device-employee mapping and raw-log import.
- Clock-in/clock-out, present, absent, leave, late and working-hour calculation.
- Basic overtime calculation using one agreed rule.
- Authorized attendance correction.
- Daily and monthly attendance reports.

## 4.5 Payroll, PF, Gratuity and Advances

- One monthly payroll group.
- Salary scale and employee salary assignment.
- Up to 10 agreed earning and deduction components.
- Attendance-based deduction using one approved formula.
- Basic overtime and festival-bonus input.
- Basic salary-advance installment deduction.
- Basic PF contribution and gratuity calculation using client-approved formulas.
- Payroll preview, approval and period lock.
- Salary sheet, department summary and individual PDF payslip.
- One bank salary sheet in an agreed Excel format.
- Cash and cheque salary sheets.
- Payslip download and optional email through a client-provided email service.

## 4.6 Basic Accounts and Finance

- Basic chart of accounts and account heads/sub-heads.
- Receipt, payment and journal vouchers.
- Money receipt and payment entry.
- Fund and petty-cash register.
- Cash and bank transaction register.
- Basic voucher approval and posting.
- General ledger and trial balance.
- Income and expenditure statement.
- Basic balance sheet based on configured accounts.
- Payroll summary posting through an agreed journal structure.

The scope covers one entity, one currency and standardized accounting workflows. Advanced budgeting, AP/AR, tax automation, reconciliation and statutory formats are future upgrades.

## 4.7 Inventory and Stores

- Product/item, category, unit and store setup.
- Product list and search.
- Requisition and one-step approval.
- Goods receipt and stock issue.
- Employee/department product assignment.
- Product return and basic stock adjustment.
- Stock ledger, current-stock and assignment reports.

## 4.8 Member Management, Billing and Portal

- Member profile, category, contact and status.
- Member list, search and Excel import.
- Basic fee setup and invoice generation.
- Payment entry, allocation and printable money receipt.
- One online-payment gateway, subject to documented API, sandbox and merchant access.
- Basic member login to view profile, invoices, payments and receipts.
- Outstanding-member and collection reports.
- Selected SMS/email notices through client-provided services.

## 4.9 BIA File and Document Register

- Registers for IDRA, Ministry, Sadharan Bima Corporation, NBR, ICC and FBCCI information.
- File/register number, subject, reference, dates and responsible officer.
- Basic inward/outward correspondence information.
- Supporting-document upload.
- Basic file movement, pending-with and due-date tracking.
- Search, file-register and pending-file reports.

## 4.10 Notifications, Dashboards and Reports

- Selected email, SMS and in-app notifications.
- Basic administrator, HR, attendance, payroll, accounts, inventory and membership summaries.
- Up to 25 agreed standard report definitions across all modules.
- PDF and Excel/CSV output where technically appropriate.
- Report access based on assigned roles.

# 5. Project Limits

The fixed price assumes:

- One organization, one policy set and one primary office/site.
- One attendance-device model/site.
- One SMS provider, one email service and one payment gateway.
- One bank salary-sheet format; direct bank API is excluded.
- Up to 25 signed-off standard report definitions.
- One trial data import and one final production import.
- English user interface with Bangla-compatible data entry.
- Standardized approval steps and business workflows.
- Responsive web application; no native mobile application.
- Client-approved payroll, PF, gratuity and accounting rules with worked examples.
- Reuse of established platform components and a consistent application design.

# 8. Client Responsibilities

BIA will:

- Nominate one authorized project coordinator and module representatives.
- Approve the basic SRS, workflows, report catalogue and acceptance cases.
- Provide all policies, payroll/PF/gratuity formulas and accounting rules with worked examples.
- Provide clean source data using agreed templates and sign migration totals.
- Provide actual devices, network access, documented SDK/API and device-vendor assistance.
- Provide SMS, email, payment merchant, hosting, domain and certificate facilities.
- Provide test users and consolidated UAT feedback within seven business days.
- Verify financial and payroll results before production acceptance.

Client-caused delays in decisions, access, data, infrastructure or UAT will result in an equivalent schedule extension.

# 9. Exclusions

- Advanced multi-level or rule-based workflow engine.
- Multiple organizations, materially different branch policies or multi-tenant SaaS.
- Advanced payroll tax, PF interest/settlement, gratuity provisioning and retroactive payroll.
- Advanced AP/AR, budgeting, bank reconciliation, fixed assets, depreciation, VAT or tax-return automation.
- Procurement, quotation, tender and purchase-order workflow.
- Advanced inventory valuation, batch, serial, warranty, expiry and barcode operation.
- Advanced member lifecycle, penalty/waiver automation, refund workflow and multiple payment gateways.
- Document versioning, OCR/full-text extraction, legal hold and retention automation.
- Active Directory/SSO, enterprise 2FA, advanced security monitoring or independent penetration testing.
- Native mobile apps, offline mode and full bilingual UI/content translation.
- Additional devices, sites, banks, gateways, providers or legacy-system integrations.
- Historical-data cleansing, scanning, OCR and manual data entry.
- Hardware, cloud/server, networking, domain, certificates, SMS/email usage and payment-gateway fees.
- High availability, separate disaster-recovery environment and 24×7 support.
- Features and reports not listed in the signed basic SRS and feature matrix.

# 10. Acceptance and Change Control

- The signed basic SRS, feature matrix, report list, formulas and agreed prototypes will form the contractual scope baseline.
- BIA will accept each milestone or provide one consolidated written list of material defects within seven business days.
- A defect is an approved requirement that fails to operate as documented. A new requirement, changed policy, additional field/workflow or new report is a change.
- Changes require written impact analysis, additional price and schedule approval before implementation.
- Device and payment integrations require a feasibility check. If a provider lacks a usable documented interface, a manual import/entry fallback will satisfy the basic Phase 1 requirement unless a separate integration is approved.

# 11. Warranty and Handover

- A 60-calendar-day defect warranty is included after production acceptance.
- Warranty covers reproducible defects within the signed scope.
- Source code and agreed technical documents will be handed over after full payment.
- Ongoing support, statutory changes and enhancements will be covered by a separate maintenance agreement or change order.
