# 9. Milestone Acceptance Procedure

1. The supplier will submit a milestone release note, deliverable checklist and available test evidence.
2. BIA will review the milestone and provide one consolidated written list of material in-scope defects within seven business days.
3. The supplier will correct verified milestone defects and resubmit the affected evidence.
4. A new feature, workflow, formula, field group, integration or report is a change request, not a defect.
5. Minor items that do not prevent operational use may be recorded for an agreed later patch without withholding acceptance of unrelated completed deliverables.
6. If BIA does not provide acceptance or a reasoned consolidated defect list within the review period, the project schedule will be extended by the delay period and assigned resources may be rescheduled.
7. Invoice payment is due within 10 calendar days of its contractual trigger or milestone submission/acceptance, as stated in the signed agreement.

# 10. Dependency and Schedule Rules

- Each milestone begins when its required BIA inputs are materially available.
- Delayed data, decisions, devices, credentials, infrastructure or UAT feedback extend the schedule by at least the resulting delay.
- External providers are responsible for their API availability, credentials, sender IDs, merchant approval and service quality.
- Where a documented third-party interface is unavailable or incompatible, the catalogue's manual import/entry fallback satisfies the basic scope unless a change order is signed.
- Work may overlap between milestones, but an invoice trigger remains governed by the signed commercial schedule.
- The combined contractual report limit remains 25 definitions.
- Future recommended upgrades are outside these milestones and require separate estimation and approval.
